About Me

I am Associate Professor in Computer Science. My academic journey began with a Ph.D. at IRCAM (STMS laboratory, ACIDS team) under the supervision of Philippe Esling and Jean-Pierre Briot, where I studied the energy consumption of neural networks applied to audio generation.

After that, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at INRIA (LORIA, Multispeech team) under the supervision of Romain Serizel, focusing on the energy footprint of AI systems for sound event detection in the the DCASE challenge.

I am now a member of the QARMA team at the LIS laboratory, continuing to explore the environmental footprint of AI systems.
